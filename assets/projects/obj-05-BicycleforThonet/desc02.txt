The primary structural frame is constructed entirely from solid beech wood. However, the inherent physical restrictions of hand-bending timber necessitated a hybrid manufacturing approach. While the sweeping macro-forms are achieved through traditional steam bending, the critical geometric nodes, complex contours, and final jointing are heavily reliant on subtractive CNC milling. To mediate the tension between the organic material and the mechanical forces of cycling, AMA introduced a series of bespoke structural connectors and sprung rods, which actively reinforce the frame's major stress areas.
