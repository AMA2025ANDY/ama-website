Aesthetically and functionally, the bicycle adheres to a strict logic of reduction. It is configured as a fixed-gear track bike—a deliberate choice intended to establish a direct, unmediated physical connection between the rider and the terrain. Stripping away extraneous mechanical components, the design features no braking system and relies on several interchangeable gear ratios. The rider's contact point is a solid beech wood saddle, suspended on a sprung rod armature. This warm, analogue timber frame is sharply contrasted by the highly technical, carbon-fibre HED H3 tri-spoke wheels, establishing a stark dichotomy between mid-century organic craft and contemporary aerodynamic composite engineering.
