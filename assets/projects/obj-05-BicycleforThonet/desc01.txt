The Thonet Concept Bike represents a critical translation of heritage manufacturing processes into a contemporary, high-performance mobility object. Commissioned by the historic German furniture company Thonet, Andy Martin Studio (AMA) was tasked with applying the brand's iconic 1930s steam-bending technique—most famously utilized in their classic 1859 cafe chair—to the structural complexities of a 21st-century bicycle frame. The project operates as an exploration of material limitations, pushing a low-tech, artisanal process into a highly engineered, stress-bearing application.
