I’ve always believed that to create real awareness, we need to speak to the masses—not just the intellectual or professional circles. And while the rhetoric around timber, sustainability, and carbon sequestration is strong in design and academic communities, it’s not filtering into mainstream understanding.
I regularly ask friends—non-architects, non-builders—what they know about timber’s role in climate solutions. The answer is usually… nothing. That’s a problem.
In my work, I’m constantly advocating for timber buildings. And I’ve seen firsthand that when clients do know about the benefits, they become the biggest advocates themselves. They spread the word. They get excited. That’s how change starts.
But here’s the thing: there’s so much attention on timber skyscrapers—the tallest, the most ambitious. While those projects are exciting, they miss the point. Most of us don’t live in towers. We live in homes, townhouses, and low-rise communities. And these are the buildings we use every single day. These are also our biggest financial, emotional, and environmental investments.
So why isn’t there more focus on building our everyday homes from timber? Why are we watching a competition to build the tallest timber tower when we should be living in timber every day?
If we’re serious about sustainability, we can’t leave timber in the realm of experimental or elite design. It needs to be part of how we build our lives—literally.
