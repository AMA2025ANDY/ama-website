The retained elevation is more than a planning gesture. By keeping the bay and its flanking brickwork, the project preserves the rhythm of the terrace as read from the pavement, while everything behind is rethought from the ground up. The early site photographs capture this in-between moment vividly: a facade propped and braced against the open sky, leaded windows catching the light with nothing behind them, and the cleared plot beyond stacked with reclaimed brick and salvaged material awaiting the new structure.

Behind the retained face, the house is rebuilt entirely in cross-laminated timber. CLT was chosen for its low embodied carbon, its precision, and above all its speed: the panels are cut to millimetre tolerance off site, delivered flat, and craned into place as a complete kit. The result is a clean and rapid erection sequence that minimises disruption on a tight suburban plot hemmed in by neighbours and mature trees.
