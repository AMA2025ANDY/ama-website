Set within a quiet residential street, the existing semi-detached property had reached the end of its useful life. Years of piecemeal alteration and structural fatigue had left the building beyond economic repair, and the decision was taken to demolish almost in its entirety. What survives is the front facade: the distinctive curved bay, its leaded glazing and rendered cheeks, retained as a fragment of the street's interwar character and as the one fixed point around which the new house is conceived.
