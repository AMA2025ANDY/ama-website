Situated in Casablanca’s Anfa District, the intervention at Villa Zevaco operates as a critical dialogue between mid-century Moroccan modernism and contemporary spatial programming. Originally designed in 1947 by Jean-François Zevaco and colloquially known as "The Butterfly Villa," the concrete structure is a masterpiece of post-war structural expressionism. Andy Martin Architecture’s (AMA) 2013 intervention strips away decades of alterations to restore the villa's domestic scale and structural purity, while seamlessly inserting a complex hospitality program. The architecture is treated as a sensitive, timeless container—a historical datum against which new tectonic elements are measured.
