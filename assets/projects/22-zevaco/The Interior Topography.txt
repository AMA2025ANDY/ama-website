Within the lounge and dining zones, the ground plane is radically transformed through the use of a bespoke, large-scale terrazzo, featuring oversized, organic black aggregate set within a stark white binder. This fluid, pebble-like flooring establishes a topographic baseline that contrasts sharply with the rigid geometries of the architectural screens and exposed masonry walls. The custom furniture—featuring sculptural, high-backed armchairs upholstered in high-contrast cream leather and muted blue velvet—acts as independent architectural objects within the space, mediating the scale between the human body and the historic concrete shell.
