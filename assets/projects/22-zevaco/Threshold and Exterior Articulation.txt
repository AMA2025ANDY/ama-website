The primary elevation is dominated by Zevaco’s monumental, sweeping concrete canopy, which operates as both a structural tour de force and a passive environmental control. AMA’s intervention formalises the approach sequence through a strict, axial reflecting pool that draws the eye toward the deeply recessed entrance. The ground-floor perimeter is articulated by rhythmic, vertical brise-soleil screens, which mitigate the harsh North African sun while establishing a geometric porosity between the interior program and the lush, curated landscape. On the upper terraces, the boundary between built form and nature is further dissolved through integrated banquette seating and organic planters carved directly into the parapet walls.
