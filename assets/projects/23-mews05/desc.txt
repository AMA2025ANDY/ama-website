The project operates as a study in mass and void, where the interior architecture feels less constructed and more excavated. The spatial sequencing is anchored by a deep, cylindrical light well—a subtractive intervention carved directly into the building’s volume. This internalized, open-air courtyard functions as an environmental lung and a central visual datum, allowing the adjacent private programs (the bedroom and bathroom) to borrow natural light and lush botanical views without compromising the strict boundary of privacy. The perimeter walls of these rooms gently curve to follow the radius of the void, creating a fluid, continuous spatial envelope that blurs traditional rectilinear boundaries.
