The material palette was chosen for restraint rather than effect. Timber carries the new rear addition — a single, consistent cladding that wraps the volume and reads as one quiet gesture against the inherited masonry of the original house. The decision to let one material do the work of the addition is deliberate: where the Edwardian fabric is layered and hierarchical, the new volume answers with a single surface and a single tone. Set close to the sea, the timber is left to weather, silvering with exposure rather than being held to a fixed finish. The material is not asked to perform or to impress. It is asked to settle, to age in step with its setting, and to mark the addition as belonging to a different register from the house it joins.
