The client's brief was a house by the sea. That setting reorders the priorities the original Edwardian plan was built around. Where the terrace was arranged for formal rooms and clear social hierarchy, a house at the coast turns outward — toward light, toward weather, toward the view and the threshold between shelter and the elements. The project holds the original house as the private register, kept to the upper levels, and moves the public life of the house into the new rear addition that opens toward the sea. The division the scheme committed to early is not an abstract move. It is the brief made spatial: the inherited fabric holds the quiet, the new volume holds the outward life the coast asks for.
