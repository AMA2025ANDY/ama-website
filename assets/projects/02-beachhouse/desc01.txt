Kensington's Edwardian terraces were built for a particular kind of life, one of formal rooms and clear hierarchies. This five-bedroom house retained little of that original grandeur beyond the generous proportions the era handed down. Rather than restore a grandeur that no longer served, the project made a decision early and held to it: the house would be divided in two. The private areas would remain confined to the original upper levels. The public life of the house would move into a new addition at the rear. The separation is deliberate, and it is legible the moment you enter and move through.
