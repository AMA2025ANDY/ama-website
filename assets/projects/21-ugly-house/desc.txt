Ironically dubbed "The Ugly House" by its architect and owner, Andy Martin, this project acts as a radical critique of London's speculative suburban housing typologies. Retaining the unremarkable, standard facade of a 1930s terraced house in Kensal Rise, the interior volume has been entirely gutted and reconfigured. The architectural intervention strips away the cellular constraints of the original floor plan, extending the rear boundary by 15 feet and introducing a dormer volume to the roof profile. This strategic expansion maximises the site's allowable density and aggressively reorients the spatial focus toward the south-facing exterior.
