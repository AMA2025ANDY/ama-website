Driven by an eco-structural agenda, the materiality of the house relies heavily on reclaimed elements and industrial components. The ground floor is anchored by reclaimed Staffordshire blue-black bricks. Beyond their raw aesthetic, these bricks serve a critical passive environmental function, acting as a thermal mass that absorbs and retains solar heat gain. Original timber floorboards were salvaged and repurposed as vertical cladding along a central structural spine, painted black to tectonically ground the space. In the kitchen area, second-hand industrial steel units and a bespoke steel island topped with white marble introduce a clinical, utilitarian contrast to the raw masonry.
