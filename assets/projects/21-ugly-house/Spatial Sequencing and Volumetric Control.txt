The internal sequence is governed by intentional volumetric compressions and releases. Martin deliberately omits traditional domestic detailing—such as architraves, skirtings, and cornicing—to heighten the purity of the geometric intersections and artificially inflate the perceived volume of the rooms. The ground plane is organised as a split-level, open-plan topography that culminates in full-height, wall-to-wall glazing. This deep structural aperture effectively dissolves the threshold between the interior living program and the external landscape.
