The manipulation of natural light acts as a primary spatial organiser. In the newly formed attic master suite, which is bounded by seamless poured-resin flooring, the architect introduces an entirely glass-walled en-suite bathroom. Constructed with raw steel frames and reeded glass, this translucent insertion provides necessary privacy while acting as a structural light well, filtering illumination down through the central stair core. Externally, the architectural programming extends to the very edge of the site boundary, where a small, grass-topped studio and sauna establish a green-roof micro-landscape. Adjacent to this, a lap pool operates on a closed-loop energy system, utilising surplus heat routed directly from the main dwelling’s central mechanical systems.
