The windowless space is anchored by a retractable roof, the design's showstopper. Drawn from the Japanese irimoya roof style and built in shou sugi ban — charred, sustainable timber — the floating ceiling opens the interior to the open sky for an al fresco experience, sunsets included when the weather allows. It is the design's answer to a site with no outward views: if the walls offer nothing, the building looks up.

The gesture suits its audience. Miami likes to eat outside, and the retractable roof delivers that open-air experience while still protecting against the city's sudden downpours. Above it, the looming presence of the 1111 Lincoln Road car park enters the composition, and indoor palms reach up toward the opening — the tropical pushing back against the charred timber overhead.
