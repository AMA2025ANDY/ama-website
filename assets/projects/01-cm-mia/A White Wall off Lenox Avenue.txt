A long, white wall in a narrow alleyway off Lenox Avenue gives almost nothing away from the street. Pedestrians could mistake it for a standard boundary and walk straight past — but that would be a mistake. The only clue is a mammoth pivoting door that punctuates the stark façade, marking the threshold between the everyday street and the world behind it.

This reticence is deliberate. The site was once a windowless courtyard, hemmed in by its urban surroundings with no desirable views to draw on. Rather than fight that condition, the design accepts it and turns it inward and upward, treating the lack of outlook as the starting point rather than a constraint to be disguised.
