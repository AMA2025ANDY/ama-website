Chotto matte means "wait a minute" in Japanese. The phrase doubles as a design principle: a measured pacing that invites diners to slow down and notice the details across the space. Chotto Matte Miami brings the brand's Tokyo energy into dialogue with the city's tropical climate and street culture. The 220-seat restaurant and bar, the first U.S. outpost of the London original, opened in Miami Beach in April 2018, serving Japanese-Peruvian Nikkei cuisine. Designed by London architectural practice ama (Andy Martin Architecture) — the firm behind all four Chotto Matte sites — the 4,500-square-foot space shifts from bright, palm-shaded dining by day to a darker, atmospheric bar by night.
