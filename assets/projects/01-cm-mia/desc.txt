Chotto matte means "wait a minute" in Japanese. The phrase doubles as a design principle: a measured pacing that invites diners to slow down and notice the details across the space. The first U.S. outpost of the London original, Chotto Matte Miami opened in April 2018, bringing the brand's Tokyo energy into dialogue with the city's tropical climate and street culture. The 220-seat restaurant and bar serves Japanese-Peruvian Nikkei cuisine across 4,500 square feet, shifting from bright, palm-shaded dining by day to a darker, atmospheric bar by night.
