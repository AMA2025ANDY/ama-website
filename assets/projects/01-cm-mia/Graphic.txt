Colour arrives through the hands of the city itself. The wraparound mural that pours over much of the interior is the work of Miami street artists Marcel Katz and Ahol Sniffs Glue, whose whimsical, graphic vocabulary carries the edge of the city's graffiti culture indoors. Set against the muted, Japanese-inflected palette of charred timber, natural stone and tinted glass, their work supplies the counterpoint the whole room is built on — the restraint of Tokyo holding hands with the exuberance of Miami, neither one winning.
