A long, white wall in a narrow alleyway off Lenox Avenue gives almost nothing away from the street. Behind it, the restaurant pairs shou sugi ban charred-timber ceilings with a boldly graphic black-and-white marble floor and lush hanging gardens. The material palette — natural stone, carbon steel, tinted glass — leans on muted, Japanese-inflected tones, then sets them against Miami's energy: most visibly in a vibrant mural that wraps around much of the room.
