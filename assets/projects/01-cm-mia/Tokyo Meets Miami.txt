Like its London counterpart, the Miami outpost carries Chotto Matte's signature urban Tokyo character, then sets it against the tropical life of its surroundings. The intention was never to copy the original but to keep the essence of the brand front and centre while drawing out the particular identity of each city it lands in. In Miami, that means palms and rock — the tropical and the geological — held in tension with the charred timber and muted tones of a Japanese sensibility.

Behind the white wall, the restaurant pairs shou sugi ban charred-timber ceilings with a boldly graphic black-and-white marble floor and lush hanging gardens, the planting designed by landscape architect Raymond Jungles. The material palette — natural stone, carbon steel, tinted glass — leans on muted, Japanese-inflected tones, then throws them against Miami's energy, most visibly in a vibrant mural that wraps around much of the room.
