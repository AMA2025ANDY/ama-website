Between the sushi bar and the cocktail area sits the room's geological heart: a 19-tonne Sicilian lavastone boulder, imported in its entirety, anchoring a cave-like 'Cenote' moment within the plan. It is the counterweight to the lightness of the opening roof — mass against sky, raw stone against refined timber.

Against this restraint, colour arrives through the work of local street artists. A vibrant mural created by Miami muralists Marcel Katz and Ahol Sniffs Glue pours over the interior walls, importing the edge of the city's graffiti culture into the room and completing the dialogue the design sets up throughout: Tokyo and Miami, charred and tropical, measured and exuberant, each holding the other in balance.
