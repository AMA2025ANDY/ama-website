The studio set out to seamlessly connect the home with its rural surroundings, basing the form on a typical agricultural barn. A steel and timber superstructure echoes the local Kent blackened barns, blending into three hectares of countryside. The end façades are clad in reclaimed chestnut — a timeless, rustic note that offsets the dominant metal-clad elevations — while the curved roof mirrors the undulating landscape to create a sense of movement between the architecture and its setting.
